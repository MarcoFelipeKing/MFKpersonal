## 4. Profile

*Paste as plain text into the rich-text editor, then apply paragraph breaks. The three paragraphs are intentional — Jadu tends to strip blank lines unless you use the editor's paragraph tool.*

---

I am a Lecturer in Building Engineering and Infection Transmission at the School of Civil Engineering, University of Leeds. I also serve as Director of the Water, Public Health and Environmental Engineering (WPE) Research Group and as Co-Director of the Healthy Buildings Network (HBN) Leeds.

My research sits at the intersection of computational fluid dynamics (CFD), epidemiology, and environmental engineering. I study how pathogens spread through the built environment — via airborne transmission, surface contact, and building ventilation systems — and develop evidence-based engineering strategies to reduce infection risk. I work with the NHS, industry partners, and international collaborators on experimental and computational studies that connect fundamental fluid mechanics to real-world public health outcomes. During the COVID-19 pandemic I contributed technical expertise to national scientific advisory groups on airborne transmission risk in buildings, informing guidance on hospital ward design, ventilation standards, and the use of air cleaning technologies.

I graduated from Durham University with a BSc in Mathematics (2008), and was awarded an MSc with distinction in Computational Fluid Dynamics from the University of Leeds (2009), where I studied the optimal placement of UV germicidal irradiation devices for the disinfection of Influenza H1N1 in hospital patient rooms. I completed my PhD at Leeds in 2013 under the supervision of Prof. Catherine Noakes and Dr. Andy Sleigh, receiving an award for excellence in research for my dissertation: "Modelling infection risk due to environmental contamination in hospital single and multi-bed ward accommodation". Previous EPSRC-funded research includes the HECOIRA project (Hospital Environment Control, Optimisation and Infection Risk Assessment), in collaboration with St James' University Teaching Hospital in Leeds, Hairmyres Hospital in Lanarkshire, Monaghans and Apex4D; and the REFRESH project (2013–2017), which coupled indoor and outdoor airflow simulation to study the effects of building environments on occupant cognitive performance.

## 6. Research Interests

*Paste section by section using the bold headings as sub-headers in the rich-text editor. All typos from the original have been corrected and outdated content updated to past tense.*

---

**Airborne Infection Transmission and Control**

Hospital-acquired infections are a major concern worldwide, costing the UK over £1,000 million per annum. My expertise is in coupling CFD simulation of microorganism-laden droplet and aerosol dispersion with infection risk epidemic models that account for stochastic human behaviour. I take a multi-scale approach to infection transmission, from individual intra-host variability through to machine learning models of patient infection at a population level, including the effects of hospital staff care patterns. I have applied CFD to examine the optimal location of air disinfection devices — including ultraviolet germicidal irradiation (UV-C) — in hospital single and four-bed wards, and continue to investigate the evidence base for single-room versus multi-bed ward design as a transmission control strategy.

**UV Disinfection and Far-UVC**

A growing area of my research concerns the use of UV-C and Far-UVC (222 nm) irradiation as a passive, continuous air disinfection strategy in occupied spaces. Far-UVC is eye- and skin-safe at guideline exposure levels, making it suitable for use while rooms are occupied. My group has published work on the modelling and real-world effectiveness of Far-UVC deployment in healthcare and public-facing environments, including a recent paper in *Building and Environment* (2025).

**Real-Time Airflow Modelling**

Within the EPSRC-funded REFRESH project (2013–2017), I worked on coupling scale-resolving CFD simulation of indoor airflow with outdoor environments. The project ran a year-long experimental campaign measuring building pressures with the University of Reading at the Silsoe research site. Outputs included methodology frameworks for comparison between CFD simulation and full-scale building ventilation and façade pressure measurements. The team developed and validated real-time Lattice-Boltzmann codes running on GPU hardware, achieving simulation speeds up to 1000 times faster than Ansys Fluent. I am a member of the Low Energy Ventilation Network (LEVN), an EPSRC UK Fluids Network Special Interest Group that sets the research agenda for low-energy building ventilation strategies.

**Air Quality Sensor Networks**

I use experimental techniques to validate temperature and CO2 air quality sensors in an environmental chamber, developing understanding of optimal sensor placement for representative indoor condition monitoring. This work informs real-world sensor network deployment in NHS and public-sector buildings.

**Statistical Inference**

I apply Directed Acyclic Graph (DAG) theory to inform causal inference in experimental design and results analysis, and am developing Bayesian inference frameworks as an alternative to deterministic approaches in engineering research. I work with the Leeds Institute for Data Analytics to expand the repertoire of statistical tools available to students and staff.

**Outreach**

I work with the Medical Research Council and the NHS to bring the concept of proper hand hygiene to visitors of all ages at the Thackray Medical Museum in Leeds. This involves interdisciplinary collaboration between mathematicians, engineers, and clinicians to create video games and interactive exercises — including glow-in-the-dark gel — that demonstrate how easily pathogens transfer between surfaces.
